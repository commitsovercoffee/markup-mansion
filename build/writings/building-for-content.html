Building Content Driven Apps

Ideas to bridge the gap between content creators & software developers.

Introduction

I have spent the last 3 year working on content rich products including a chatbot, a learning management system and few static websites. While working on these, I got the chance to work with content creators from diverse backgrounds, who were trying to solve something specific.

Interestingly, despite niche requirements, some patterns and anti-patterns were common across projects and teams. As a developer, I have often experienced push back from content creators on certain approaches/practices. And while we are able to come to a resolution later, we often miss to discuss the approach in detail due to :

  • Time constraint.
  • Technical jargon.

Knowing why something worked (patterns) is as important as knowing why something didn’t or won’t (anti-patterns). Hence, this blog is my attempt in the same direction.

I have tried to keep the techno-blabber to a minimum & included examples. Since this is a blog, you can read it later & can’t avoid the discussion anymore. Haha, gotcha !

P.S Most of these patterns are just (not so) common sense.

Writing Content

Let’s start at the basics. Why ? Because more people tend to overlook these than you’d expect. If you know these, skim through or have a quick read or use this as a checklist.

Unlike writing novels or books, when you are writing content for an app or website, there needs to be some structure, which may not be clear initially, but you need to arrive at one at some point. Until that time, I think starting off with the basics isn’t a bad idea.

Writing is subjective. My attempt here is not to impose my style or approach on you, but ensure that the content meets a baseline standard of quality.

Structure & Emphasis

Adding structure and emphasis in content writing is crucial for clarity, coherence, and effective communication. Without these the content would be incomplete.

  1. Structure : Create a structure for your document.

    A well-structured document guides readers through the content. Headings, subheadings, bullet points etc help readers understand the flow of information. Such a structured document is also easier to collaborate on.

    • Consider structure of this blog as an example. Do you think it’d be as easy to navigate through this blog without headings, bullet points and rules ?

    • Do you use google docs ? Do you use the formattng options ? Why not !

    Without the desired formatting, the structure maybe obvious to you as the author, but it isn’t to others and without an obvious flow, working on someone else’s doc becomes inefficient and thus tedious. And this in-efficiency compounds. Collaborating

    There’s even a book dedicated to the principle of ”Don’t make me think”, aimed at designing web interfaces, yet its principles are equally applicable in this context.

  2. Emphasize : This is more art than science. (See what I did there?)

    A well-placed emphasis, like bolding or italics, draws attention to crucial information. This helps readers distinguish the main takeaways from supporting details.

    • The sales figures for Q3 were significantly lower than expected.
    • The sales figures for Q3 were significantly lower than expected.

Adding Tone

Adding tone in content writing is essential for conveying the intended attitude, emotion, and personality to engage and connect with the audience effectively.

The tone in writing depends on the style chosen, making it optional.

  1. Emoticons

    A strategically placed emoticon can highlight a specific point, but it’s important to be mindful of their limitations as they may be open to misinterpretation.

    • I’m really sorry to hear about your loss.
    • I’m really sorry to hear about your loss. 😔

  2. Figures of Speech

    Humor, metaphors, similes, and other figures of speech can add personality, tone, and clarity to your writing.

    • ✅ The runners zipped past like cheetahs.
    • ❌ The race was fast.

  3. Word Choice

    Strong verbs, vivid adjectives, and powerful nouns can effectively convey emotions and emphasis.

    • ✅ The tired old dog dragged its paws slowly down the street.
    • ❌ The tired old dog walked slowly down the street.
