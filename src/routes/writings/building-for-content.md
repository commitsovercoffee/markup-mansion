## Introduction

I have spent the last 3 year working on content rich products including a chatbot,
a learning management system and few static websites. While working on these,
I got the chance to work with content creators from diverse backgrounds,
who were trying to solve something specific.

Interestingly, despite niche requirements, some patterns and anti-patterns
were common across projects and teams. As a developer, I have often experienced
push back from content creators on certain approaches/practices. And while we
are able to come to a resolution later, we often miss to discuss the approach in
detail due to :

- Time constraint.
- Technical jargon.

Knowing why something worked (patterns) is as important as knowing why something
didn't or won't (anti-patterns). Hence, this blog is my attempt in the same direction.

I have tried to keep the techno-blabber to a minimum & included examples.
Since this is a blog, you can read it _later_ & can't avoid the discussion anymore. Haha, gotcha !

P.S Most of these patterns are just (not so) common sense.

## Structure & Emphasis

1. Structure : Create a structure for your document.

   A well-structured document guides readers through the content. Headings, subheadings,
   bullet points etc help readers understand the flow of information and find specific
   details quickly. And is also easier to edit and revise. You can focus on specific sections without getting lost in the overall flow.

   - Consider structure of this blog, for example.

2. Emphasize : This is more **art** than _science_. (See what I did there?)

   A well-placed emphasis, like bolding or italics, draws attention to crucial information.
   This helps readers distinguish the main takeaways from supporting details.

   - The **sales figures for Q3** were significantly lower than expected.
   - The sales figures for Q3 were significantly lower than expected.

## Adding Tone

1. Emoticons

   A strategically placed emoticon can highlight a specific point but it's important to be mindful of their limitations.

   - I'm really sorry to hear about your loss.
   - I'm really sorry to hear about your loss. 😔

2. Figures of Speech

   Humor, metaphors, similes, and other figures of speech can add personality, tone, and clarity to your writing.

   - ✅ The runners zipped past like cheetahs.
   - ❌ The race was fast.

3. Word Choice

   Strong verbs, vivid adjectives, and powerful nouns can effectively convey emotions and emphasis.

   - ✅ The tired old dog **dragged** its paws slowly down the street.
   - ❌ The tired old dog walked slowly down the street.
